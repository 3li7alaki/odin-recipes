Shawarma Recipe

Description

Shawarma is a beloved Middle Eastern dish, especially popular in Syria. This recipe features succulent slices of marinated meat, often lamb or chicken, cooked on a vertical rotisserie. The meat is shaved off and served in pita bread, along with fresh vegetables like tomatoes, cucumbers, and onions. A generous drizzle of tahini or garlic sauce completes the dish.

The marinade for Syrian Shawarma often includes a blend of spices such as cumin, paprika, and cardamom, which infuses the meat with rich flavors. It's a perfect dish for sharing with friends and family, enjoyed with side dishes like hummus or tabbouleh.

Ingredients

  • 500g of lamb or chicken (thinly sliced)
  • 2 cloves of garlic (minced)
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Pita bread
  • Fresh vegetables (tomatoes, cucumbers, onions)
  • Tahini or garlic sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix the olive oil, minced garlic, lemon juice, and all the spices (cumin, paprika, cardamom, cinnamon, salt, and pepper).
  2. Add the thinly sliced meat to the marinade, making sure all the pieces are coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for better flavor.
  3. Heat a pan over medium-high heat, then cook the marinated meat until fully cooked and slightly crispy around the edges.
  4. Warm the pita bread in the oven or on a pan.
  5. Assemble the shawarma by adding the cooked meat to the pita bread, along with fresh vegetables and a drizzle of tahini or garlic sauce.
  6. Roll or fold the pita, and enjoy your homemade Syrian Shawarma!
